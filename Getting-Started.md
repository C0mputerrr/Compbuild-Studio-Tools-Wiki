# Getting Started

## Open the editor

Press **Home** to open the docked editor: a **tool rail** on the left, a
**Tool Options** panel on the right, and a status bar. The world stays visible
behind the panels — it's a viewport, not a menu. Press **Esc** or **Home**
again to close it (and the in-world selection/clipboard visuals vanish while
it's closed — they come back when you reopen).

## Look vs. cursor

While the editor is open you're either **flying/looking** or using the
**cursor**:

- **Move the mouse** to look around (free camera). **W A S D** move,
  **Space / Shift** go up/down, **Left-Ctrl** is faster.
- Press **Esc** (or tap **Left-Alt**) to release the **cursor** so you can click
  panels and blocks. **Esc** again returns to flying; a third **Esc** closes the
  editor.

There's no crosshair — the **block you're aiming at is outlined**, and that's
your pointer. Terrain/paint tools show a **brush ring** there too.

## Your first edits

**Fill a box**

1. Aim at one corner block and press **B** (or click it in cursor mode).
2. Aim at the opposite corner and press **B** again — chat shows the size.
3. Hold a block (or pick a material) and press **F** to fill. **G** clears to
   air; **R** re-skins non-air blocks.

**Copy & paste**

1. With a selection, press **C** to copy.
2. Look where you want it and press **V** — or plant the ghost with a click to
   position it with gizmos, then place.

**Undo anything**

- **Ctrl+Z** undoes; **Ctrl+Y** redoes. Every edit is reversible.

## Pick a material

Open the **MATERIAL** tab (top-right) and click the material row to open the
**picker** — type to search, click a block. **Air is the first entry** (it
erases). Your last 10 materials live in the **RECENT** tab, and you can save
**named palettes** — see **[[Materials, Palettes & Presets]]**.

## Try a tool

Click a tool in the rail (hover any icon for its name), then read its
one‑line **?** description on the Tool Options title bar. Good first tools:

- **Sculpt** — point at the ground and click/hold to raise terrain.
- **Path** — click points to lay a road; **Build path** commits it.
- **Transform** — copy something, then rotate/scale the ghost and click to place.

Full details in **[[Tools]]** and **[[Controls]]**.
