# Top Bar & Settings

The top bar has four menus, plus the tool rail and status bar.

## File

- **Import schematic…** — the thumbnail browser ([[Schematics]]).
- **Save clipboard as .schem…** — export the clipboard.
- **Open schematics folder** — open `<game>/schematics` in your OS file browser.

## Edit

The full edit set, grouped with its shortcuts:

- **Undo** / **Redo**
- **Cut** / **Copy** / **Paste**
- **Fill selection** / **Delete to air** / **Replace with held**
- **Clear selection** / **Drop clipboard**

## Settings

Editor-wide view preferences:

- **Look speed** — mouse sensitivity while flying.
- **UI scale** — panel density / crispness (1–4).
- **Gizmos** — Shown/Hidden. Hides the in-world selection/brush overlays for a
  clean screenshot. *Stroking still applies edits while hidden.*
- **Ghost preview** — Shown/Hidden. Hides the clipboard/path build previews.
- **Restore view defaults** — reset the above.
- **Font** — whether the bundled **Manrope** font bound, or the vanilla
  fallback (a diagnostic row).
- **Server** — the multiplayer link status: `linked — plugin vX`,
  `refused — …`, `no reply …`, or `n/a (singleplayer)`. See
  [[Multiplayer (Paper)]].

> Closing the editor hides **all** in-world editor visuals (selection, clipboard
> ghost, path/geometry/slope/gradient previews). Nothing is deleted — reopening
> restores them.

## Help

- **Controls…** — a modal cheat-sheet of every key and click (Esc/click closes).
- **Tool guide: the ? button** — points you at the per-tool **?** descriptions.
- **About** — name, version, and the beta notice.

## Tool Options & the tool rail

The right **Tool Options** panel shows the active tool's settings (draggable
sliders, steppers, cycle chips), a **?** description, and — for Sculpt/Paint/
Biome — three **preset slots** ([[Materials, Palettes & Presets]]).

The **rail** is a floating grid that wraps into more columns so every tool is
visible at any resolution. **Drag the white logo** to move it; **drag its right
edge** to enlarge the icons. Hover any icon for its name.

The **right inspector** is resizable — **drag its left edge** to widen or narrow
it, so a cut-off label or value (e.g. in PROPS) gets room.
